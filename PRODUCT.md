# Product

## Register

product

## Users

People pursuing financial independence who feel the weight of their current situation: Sunday scaries, burnout, the paycheck treadmill. They are not finance experts and should not need to be. They want hope, clarity, and proof that each step matters — that savings are meaningful and progress is real, even when the journey is long.

## Product Purpose

Fire is a personal finance companion for the FIRE marathon. It makes independence feel tangible: celebrating wins along the way (bills paid off, first green month, five- and six-figure milestones, when the 4% rule covers a fixed expense), showing how much closer each step brings them, and lifting the anxiety of "am I even on track?" Planning and projection tools exist for power users but stay progressively disclosed; for most people, the journey — not the spreadsheet — is the product.

## Brand Personality

Warm, hopeful, celebratory, encouraging. The emotional register sits between Headspace and Monarch: as fluid and calming as the former, as grounded and trustworthy as the latter — but less "woo-woo floaty" than wellness apps and less cold or institutional than traditional finance tools.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dashboards (navy gradients, hero metrics, identical card grids)
- Corporate banking apps (sterile, institutional, compliance-heavy UI)
- High-tech fintech that feels competitive, gamified, or hustle-culture driven
- Spreadsheet-first power-user tools where jargon and density come before understanding
- Anything that adds anxiety instead of lifting it

## Design Principles

1. **Journey over jargon** — Progress should feel real without requiring finance expertise. The 4% rule, lean/coast/barista FIRE, and milestone math should land as hope, not homework.
2. **Celebrate wins, not just totals** — Milestones matter as much as net worth: paid-off bills, green months, digit thresholds, and "your portfolio could cover X" moments deserve visible recognition.
3. **Weight lifted, not weight added** — Every screen should reduce anxiety. Down markets still tell an honest story; the math should make sense even when feelings don't.
4. **Progressive disclosure** — Depth for planners and power users lives underneath; the default path stays simple, encouraging, and journey-first.
5. **Honest math, gentle tone** — Numbers are precise; voice is warm. Never sacrifice accuracy for cheerleading, and never let accuracy feel punitive.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA across all product surfaces. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for celebrations and transitions so milestone moments never depend on animation to communicate meaning.
